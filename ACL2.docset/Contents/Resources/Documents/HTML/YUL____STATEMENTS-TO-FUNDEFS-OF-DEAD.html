Correctness theorem for statements-to-fundefs.

This proof is a bit more elaborate than others because of the afterp flag of statement-list-dead. To have a sufficiently strong induction hypothesis we need that flag to be generic, not to have a specific value. Thus, we introduce a predicate universally quantified over afterp, and we prove that it holds by induction. There may be a way to avoid the explicit quantification, particularly because we are only interested in two values of afterp, namely nil and non-nil.

Theorem: statements-to-fundefs-of-dead

(defthm
   statements-to-fundefs-of-dead
   (equal (statements-to-fundefs (statement-list-dead stmts afterp))
          (fundef-list-dead (statements-to-fundefs stmts))))
