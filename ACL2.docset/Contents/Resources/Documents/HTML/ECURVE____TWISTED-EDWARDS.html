Elliptic curves over prime fields in twisted Edwards form.

Twisted Edwards curves are introduced in Bernstein, Birkner, Joye, Lange, and Peters's ``Twisted Edwards Curves''this paper.

Their definition is the following:

Definition 2.1. (Twisted Edwards curve). Fix a field k with \mathrm{char}(k) \neq 2. Fix distinct nonzero elements a, d \in k. The twisted Edwards curve with coefficients a and d is the curve

\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{E},a,d} : a x^2 + y^2 = 1 + d x^2 y^2

An Edwards curve is a twisted Edwards curve with a = 1.

Since, as noted above, Edwards curves are a special case of twisted Edwards curves, our formalization of twisted Edwards curves also covers Edwards curves, at least for some purposes.
