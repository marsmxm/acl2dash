Evaluate a tame expression using apply$

When apply$, actually apply$-lambda, is asked to apply a LAMBDA object to some arguments it calls ev$ on the body of the object and an alist binding the formals of the object to the arguments. Roughly put, ev$ ``works'' by looking up symbols, returning quoted objects, and using apply$ to apply function symbols to the results of evaluating their arguments. Ev$ and its clique-mate ev$-list are mutually-recursive with apply$. Ev$ can only evaluate ``as expected'' on tame expressions and requires warrants, explicit in the proof theory or implicit in the evaluation theory, to determine badges and thus tameness. See apply$ for details, including the formal definitions of ev$ and ev$-list.
