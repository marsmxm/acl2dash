Lemmas useful to prove some of the theorems about set-size.

The lemma len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-subsetp is used to prove the theorem set-size-when-subsetp, and the lemma len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-strict-subsetp is similarly used to prove the theorem set-size-when-strict-subsetp. set-size is defined as len of remove-duplicates, which produces lists satisfying no-duplicatesp, making the lemmas applicable to the theorems.

Definitions and Theorems

Theorem: len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-subsetp

(defthm len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-subsetp
        (implies (and (no-duplicatesp-equal x)
                      (subsetp-equal x y))
                 (<= (len x) (len y))))

Theorem: len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-strict-subsetp

(defthm len-when-no-duplicatesp-and-strict-subsetp
        (implies (and (no-duplicatesp-equal x)
                      (subsetp-equal x y)
                      (not (subsetp-equal y x)))
                 (< (len x) (len y))))
