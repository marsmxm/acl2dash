(maybe-integerp-equiv x y) is an equivalence relation for maybe-integerps, i.e., equality up to maybe-integerp-fix.

Performance note. In the execution, this is just an inlined call of eql.

Definitions and Theorems

Function: maybe-integer-equiv$inline

(defun maybe-integer-equiv$inline (x y)
       (declare (xargs :guard (and (maybe-integerp x)
                                   (maybe-integerp y))))
       (eql (maybe-integerp-fix x)
            (maybe-integerp-fix y)))

Theorem: maybe-integer-equiv-is-an-equivalence

(defthm maybe-integer-equiv-is-an-equivalence
        (and (booleanp (maybe-integer-equiv x y))
             (maybe-integer-equiv x x)
             (implies (maybe-integer-equiv x y)
                      (maybe-integer-equiv y x))
             (implies (and (maybe-integer-equiv x y)
                           (maybe-integer-equiv y z))
                      (maybe-integer-equiv x z)))
        :rule-classes (:equivalence))
