(aig-or x1 x2 ...) constructs an AIG representing (or x1 x2 ...).

Like aig-and, we attempt to lazily avoid computing later terms in the expression.

Macro: aig-or

(defmacro
     aig-or (&rest args)
     (cons 'mbe
           (cons ':logic
                 (cons (aig-or-macro-logic-part args)
                       (cons ':exec
                             (cons (aig-or-macro-exec-part args)
                                   'nil))))))
