Notion of redundancy for APT transformations.

A call of an APT transformation is redundant if and only if it is identical to a previous successful call of the same transformation whose :show-only input is not t, except that the two calls may differ in their :print and :show-only inputs. These options do not affect the generated events, and thus they are ignored for the purpose of redundancy.

A call of an APT transformation whose :show-only input is t does not generate any event. No successive call may be redundant with such a call.
