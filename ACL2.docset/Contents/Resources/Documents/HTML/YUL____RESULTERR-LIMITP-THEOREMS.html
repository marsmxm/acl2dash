Theorems about resulterr-limitp.

These are mainly about certain dynamic semantic operations never returning limit errors. There is also one theorem to simplify resulterr-limitp when applied to resulterr. There is also a theorem to show that an error is not a limit error, based on looking at the keyword (assuming it is constant).

Definitions and Theorems

Theorem: resulterr-limitp-of-resulterr-of-info

(defthm
 resulterr-limitp-of-resulterr-of-info
 (implies
   (and (resulterrp error)
        (error-info-wfp error))
   (equal (resulterr-limitp
               (resulterr (cons more (fty::resulterr->info error))))
          (resulterr-limitp error))))

Theorem: not-resulterr-limitp-of-const

(defthm
 not-resulterr-limitp-of-const
 (implies (and (syntaxp (quotep kwd))
               (not (equal kwd :limit)))
          (not (resulterr-limitp
                    (resulterr (list (list fn (cons kwd more))))))))

Theorem: not-resulterr-limitp-of-eval-literal

(defthm not-resulterr-limitp-of-eval-literal
