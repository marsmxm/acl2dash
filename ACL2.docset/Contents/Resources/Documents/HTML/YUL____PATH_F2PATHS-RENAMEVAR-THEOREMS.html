Theorems about variable renaming for paths.

If two paths or lists of paths are related by variable renaming, extracting their underlying variables causes no errors. This is what the first two theorems below say.

If two paths or lists of paths are related by variable renaming, so are the underlying variables. This is what the last two theorems below say.

Definitions and Theorems

Theorem: path-to-var-not-error-when-path-renamevar

(defthm path-to-var-not-error-when-path-renamevar
        (implies (not (resulterrp (path-renamevar old new ren)))
                 (and (not (resulterrp (path-to-var old)))
                      (not (resulterrp (path-to-var new))))))

Theorem: paths-to-vars-not-error-when-path-list-renamevar

(defthm
     paths-to-vars-not-error-when-path-list-renamevar
     (implies (not (resulterrp (path-list-renamevar old new ren)))
              (and (not (resulterrp (paths-to-vars old)))
                   (not (resulterrp (paths-to-vars new))))))

Theorem: var-renamevar-not-error-when-path-renamevar

(defthm var-renamevar-not-error-when-path-renamevar
        (implies (not (resulterrp (path-renamevar old new ren)))
                 (not (resulterrp (var-renamevar (path-to-var old)
                                                 (path-to-var new)
                                                 ren)))))

Theorem: var-list-renamevar-not-error-when-path-list-renamevar

(defthm
   var-list-renamevar-not-error-when-path-list-renamevar
   (implies (not (resulterrp (path-list-renamevar old new ren)))
            (not (resulterrp (var-list-renamevar (paths-to-vars old)
                                                 (paths-to-vars new)
                                                 ren)))))
