Theorem related to the exceptional points in the birational mapping.

Theorem ecurve::montgomery-only-point-with-y-0-when-aa-minus-4-non-square limits certain exceptional points in the birational mapping under a certain condition on the Montgomery curve. Here we show that Jubjub, in Montgomery form, satisfies that condition, i.e. that A^2 - 4 is not a square.

Definitions and Theorems

Theorem: not-pfield-squarep-of-jubjub-montgomery-a-square-minus-4

(defthm
    not-pfield-squarep-of-jubjub-montgomery-a-square-minus-4
    (b* ((a (ecurve::montgomery-curve->a (jubjub-montgomery-curve)))
         (a^2-4 (sub (mul a a (jubjub-q))
                     4 (jubjub-q))))
        (not (ecurve::pfield-squarep a^2-4 (jubjub-q)))))
