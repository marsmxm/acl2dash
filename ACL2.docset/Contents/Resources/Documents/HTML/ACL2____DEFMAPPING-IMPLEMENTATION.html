Implementation of defmapping.

The implementation functions have arguments, as well as results (in the :returns specifiers), consistently named as follows:

  • state is the ACL2 state.
  • wrld is the ACL2 world.
  • ctx is the context used for errors.
  • name, doma, domb, alpha, beta, beta-of-alpha-thm, alpha-of-beta-thm, guard-thms, unconditional, thm-names, hints, print, and show-only are the homonymous inputs to defmapping, before being validated. These variables have no types because they may be any values.
  • name$, doma$, domb$, alpha$, beta$, beta-of-alpha-thm$, alpha-of-beta-thm$, guard-thms$, unconditional$, thm-name$, hints$, print$, and show-only$ are the results of processing the homonymous inputs (without the $) to defmapping. Some are identical to the corresponding inputs, but they have types implied by their successful validation, performed when they are processed.
  • call is the call of defmapping supplied by the user.
  • call$ is the result of removing :print and :show-only from call.
  • expansion is the encapsulate generated by defmapping.
  • a1...an is the list of variables a1, ..., an described in the documentation.
  • b1...bm is the list of variables b1, ..., bm described in the documentation.

Implementation functions' arguments and results that are not listed above are described in, or clear from, those functions' documentation.
